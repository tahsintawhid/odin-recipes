Brownie Recipe

Description

This is a recipe for making Fudgy Brownies which are square-shaped and contain chocolate. The brownies were developed in the United States during the late-19th century and early 20th century, and are now popular all across the country, and even worldwide. Brownies can be served with milk or ice cream, but they are also tasty on their own.

Ingredients

  • All-Purpose Flour
  • Butter
  • Sugar
  • Cocoa Powder
  • Eggs
  • Pure Vanilla Extract
  • Salt

    • Steps

    1. Melt the butter with sugar, salt, and cocoa powder. This should be done with the butter, sugar, salt, and cocoa powder being mixed in a bowl, with the bowl being above a saucepan with an inch of water on low heat. This way the ingredients can mix and add texture to the brownies.
    2. Add the vanilla extract and the eggs. The vanilla extract and the eggs should be added with the bowl containing butter, sugar, salt, and cocoa powder and should be mixed until the eggs and vanilla extract are entirely mixed with the mixture.
    3. Add the flour. The flour should be mixed into the batter, and then beaten so that the batter is very thick, which is important for the butter to be spread across the batter, which helps the brownies when they are baked.
    4. Bake the brownies. The brownies should be baked in the oven for 20 to 25 minutes until the brownies are solid, but also somewhat soft in the middle.
    5. Let it cool on the pan. Let the brownies cool for about 5 to 10 minutes before you put them elsewhere.
    6. Enjoy your brownies! You can also pair your brownies with a glass of warm milk or with ice cream.
