Cookie Recipe

Description

This is a recipe for making Chocolate Chip Cookies which are a classic cookie that was made in the United States and is now eaten and sold everywhere around the world. These cookies are usually paired with milk and this pairing is also associated with Santa Claus and Christmas.

Ingredients

  • All-Purpose Flour
  • Baking Soda
  • Light Brown Sugar and White Sugar
  • Unsalted Butter
  • Kosher Salt and Flaked Sea Salt
  • Eggs
  • Pure Vanilla Extract
  • Semisweet Chocolate Chips

    • Steps

    1. Soften butter. The butter needs to be soft so that it can cream with the sugars well.
    2. Whisk together dry ingredients. Carefully measure flour, baking soda, and salt using measuring cups.
    3. Cream butter and sugars. It should take about 3 minutes for butter and sugars to be light, fluffy, and very pale in color.
    4. Add eggs and vanilla extract. Mix eggs and vanilla extract with the butter and sugar mixture with the mixer being on low.
    5. Add dry ingredients. Add dry ingredients and mix on low until they're fully combined and don't overmix.
    6. Refrigerate 20 minutes. Chill the dough for 20 minutes while the oven heats up so that the center is soft as the cookie is baked.
    7. Bake at 350Â°F for 9 to 12 minutes. Use a heaping tablespoon to measure out the cookie dough onto the baking sheet.
    8. Deflate cookies. Firmly tap the baking sheet on the counter to deflate the cookies and keep the centers soft.
    9. Let it cool on the pan. Let the cookies cool for about 5 to 10 minutes before you put them elsewhere.
    10. Enjoy your cookies! You can also pair your cookies with a glass of warm milk and treat yourself the way Santa Claus would.
